The Challenge

ref5esh began as a way to capitalize on what is generally regarded as scrap, or trash, by transforming the materials into functional decor. The mission became: repurpose with real purpose. The challenge was marketing products to fortify sales.

The most successful advertising came from a consumer's ability to see the products in person.

the solution

Based on the case studies I performed, it became apparent that interest peaked in seeing the products in a real living environment, as opposed to a sales floor. Alternative methods of selling products became the best option; to sell from bars, coffee shops, and other locations where they could be used before ordering one of your own.

Meanwhile, my involvement in local non-profits became a fine enough cause for donating ref5esh products.

Printed descriptions are used to articulate the sophisticated nature of repurposed materials.

The impact of donating my hard work, for the benefit of an organization which has so much of my emotional investment already, became a driving-force for the continuation of my efforts to benefit them.

The Result

Today, ref5esh remains an identity which works to minimize the carbon footprint, while also benefiting local non-profits.
